PSYCHOLOGY INSIGHTS

The emotional and behavioral forces behind the data — why people swipe, when, and what it actually means.

🌆

The Evening Spike

Activity surges 6–9 PM not by accident. After a full workday, users seek distraction and social validation. The brain's reward system is more receptive to novelty when slightly fatigued — making swipes feel effortlessly satisfying.

💬

High Engagement ≠ Real Relationships

553K swipes. 52K matches. Only 288 relationships. The dopamine loop of swiping substitutes for the actual effort of building a real connection. Engagement metrics serve the platform — not the user.

⚖️

The Gender Asymmetry Effect

75% male users means women receive disproportionate attention — causing choice paralysis and message fatigue. Men swipe broadly and indiscriminately to compensate, which lowers their own match quality and morale over time.

📍

Proximity as Psychological Safety

Matching within 10 miles is not just convenience. It reduces perceived risk. A nearby match feels more real, more accountable. Distance is a subconscious filter for how serious someone actually is.
