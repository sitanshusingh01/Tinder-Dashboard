PSYCHOLOGY INSIGHTS

The emotional and behavioral forces behind the data — why people swipe, when, and what it actually means.

🌆

The Evening Spike

Activity surges between 6 PM and 9 PM. This is not coincidence. After a full workday, users seek distraction, validation, and social connection. The brain's reward system is more receptive to novelty in the evening, making swipes feel more satisfying.

💬

High Engagement ≠ Real Relationships

Despite 553K swipes and 52K matches, only 288 relationships formed. High digital engagement creates the illusion of social richness without the substance. The dopamine loop of swiping substitutes for the effort of building real connection.

⚖️

The Gender Asymmetry Effect

With 75% male users, women receive disproportionate attention, leading to choice paralysis and message fatigue. Men, facing intense competition, swipe broadly and indiscriminately — a strategy that lowers their own match quality and morale.

📍

Proximity as Psychological Safety

Matching within 10 miles is not just convenience. It reduces uncertainty and perceived risk. A nearby match feels more real, more accountable. Distance is a subconscious filter for seriousness of intent.
